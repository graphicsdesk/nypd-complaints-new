Protests & RDI

Feb. 26, 2012

Trayvon Martin, a 17-year old Black teenager, is shot in Florida by neighborhood watch volunteer George Zimmerman.

Mar. 9 - Apr. 6, 2012

Protestors across the country take to the streets in response to Martin's murder, calling for Zimmerman's arrest.

RDI

RDI trends in CD 9 reached 1.50 almost immediately before Martin's death. However, once the protests began, the RDI decreased significantly. It is unclear whether the protests were the sole cause of the dip in RDI (and thus, a dip in the arrests and complaints filed against Black people within CD 9). The RDI also appears to slowly increase after the first few weeks of protests.

This suggests that although the protests may minimize the arrest/ complaint numbers of Black people, the trend does not last.

Jul. 13, 2013

Zimmerman is acquitted in the death of Trayvon Martin.

Jul. 14, 2013

Countrywide protests resurge as protestors demand justice for Martin.

RDI

Although CD 9's RDI decreases to 0.9 during the first week of protest,

this dip in the arrest of Black people does not last either.

Manhattan's overall RDI is more difficult to explain due to its more volatile fluctuations.

Jul. 17, 2014

Eric Garner, a 43-year old Black man, is choked & killed by NYPD officer Daniel Pantaleo.

Aug. 9, 2014

Michael Brown, an 18-year-old Black man, is shot and killed by Ferguson police officer Darren Wilson.

Jul. to Aug. 2014

Protestors demand justice for Garner and Brown.

Nov. 24 and Dec. 4

The St. Louis grand jury decides not to indict Wilson, while the Richmond County grand jury decides not to indict Pantaleo. Protests intensify.

RDI

In CD 9, the RDI trend is more unique than in previous protest periods. Rather than decline, the RDI trend maintains a plateau until it finally dips between the second and third weeks of protest. This dip occurs when Eric Garnerâ€™s grand jury decision is announced,

suggesting that the unrest caused by both events possibly led to the decrease in arrests against Black people in the following weeks.

Apr. 12, 2015

Freddie Gray, a 25-year old Black man, is taken into police custody in Baltimore, Maryland. He sustains severe spinal cord injuries while in custody.

Apr. 19, 2015

Freddie Gray dies in a hospital as a result of the injuries sustained while in police custody.

Apr. 18, 2015

Protests calling for justice for Gray spread all across the country.

RDI

The number of arrests and complaints against Black people were already fluctuating weeks prior to the protests, suggesting again that protests may not have played a significant factor in decreasing the numbers.

May 25, 2020

George Floyd, a 46-year old Black man, is killed by Minneapolis police officers.

May 26, 2020 - present

Protests across the country continue to honor Floyd & demand an end to systemic racism.

RDI

CD 9's RDI trend around the first weeks of protest honouring Floyd are difficult to interpret. Although the RDI is less than 1.00 during this period, it had already reached this value in the weeks before. While taking other factors into account,

it appears that the protests are failing to alleviate the criminalisation of Black people, as the RDI increases once again.
